# Brief: Navigating Latent Space

**Status:** Done (published: `_posts/2026-05-26-navigating-latent-space.md`)
**Author:** pjsvis
**Date:** 2026-05-23

## Concept

A practical, opinionated guide to navigating AI agent sessions — staying on path, recognizing when you're lost, recovering from bad states, and understanding the "fucked-adjacent" zone.

## Thesis

Working with an AI agent in a long session is a navigation problem. You are moving through a high-dimensional space of possible outputs, and most paths lead to noise. This post gives the reader a compass.

## Structure

1. **Opening** — The experiential hook. What it feels like to be lost in a session. Don't explain latent space technically; make the reader feel it.
2. **Staying on Path** — Pre-session intent clarity, session structure, keeping receipts (documenting what works).
3. **Recognizing the Weeds** — Signals: repetition, confident nonsense, output collapse, context window filling with garbage.
4. **Getting Out** — Recovery options: reset, dump context, switch model, reformulate. Explicit tradeoffs.
5. **Fucked-Adjacent** — The specific state where you're still moving but not productive. How to recognize it. What to do.
6. **Closing** — This is a learnable skill. Treat it like one.

## Tone

World-weary, direct, opinionated. No hedging on advice. The reader is an experienced peer.
