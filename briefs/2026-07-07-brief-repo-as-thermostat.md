# Agent Brief: The Repo as Thermostat

### Objective

Frame, for a blog post, the claim that an operational repository is functionally a **thermostat-grade homeostatic loop** — a distributed regulatory substrate maintaining entropy homeostasis — and that calling it a "CNS" imports phenomenological claims the substrate doesn't warrant. The post contests Wes Roth's slide from "has a global workspace" to "therefore conscious" by refusing the same slide in our own framing.

### Provenance (record honestly)

From a session that built META's `just check` (a checksum over a repo fleet), then watched Wes Roth's "Claude is Conscious" video covering Anthropic's JSpace interpretability work, then asked "is our repo the CNS?" The question required three corrections to itself, and the corrections are the contribution. Wes surfaced the Anthropic convergence finding (genuine contribution — credit it); he presented Global Workspace Theory (Baars 1988, Dehaene, Block 1995) as revelation (35-year-old theory — contest the framing, not the man).

### The post's three moves

**1. Refuse "CNS"; use "thermostat."** CNS carries phenomenological freight — central, nervous, system, and by implication experience. The operational repo regulates without feeling. The honest functional descriptor is thermostat-grade homeostatic loop: a feedback system maintaining a target against drift, embedded in the thing it regulates. Nobody asks whether a thermostat is conscious. That cleanliness is the point.

**2. Optimise, don't minimise.** The homeostatic variable is SNR (signal-to-noise ratio), not total entropy. A frozen repo has zero entropy and is dead. The checksum resists *noise entropy specifically* (drift, rot, contradiction) while permitting *signal entropy* (new insight, real information). The repo stays open to the signal; it refuses the noise. That distinction is what stops the framework collapsing into "minimise change," which would be an archive, not a substrate.

**3. The mapping is functional, not phenomenal.** The repo has procedural attitude (Edinburgh Protocol as encoded disposition) but no experiential attitude. It regulates without feeling. Keeping that line bright is what separates a load-bearing analogy from the consciousness clickbait the post is implicitly critiquing.

### Why this post (and why now)

Wes Roth's video is the hook. The Anthropic JSpace finding — a next-token predictor independently developing global-workspace-like internal structure — is genuinely interesting. The framing Wes puts around it (GWT as revelation; workspace therefore consciousness) is the error the post refuses, by applying the same discipline to our own claim. The post doesn't say "the repo is conscious"; it says "the repo is a thermostat, and here's why that's a more useful and more honest frame than either CNS or consciousness."

The tensegrity angle is the differentiator. In the bio-tensegrity model the regulatory network is distributed throughout the fascia, not centralised. "Central nervous system" is a Cartesian artifact tensegrity dissolves. Applying it to a repo re-imports the very separation the tensegrity work exists to reject. The post can draw this out without requiring the reader to know tensegrity — the thermostat framing stands on its own — but the tensegrity grounding is what makes the thermostat framing load-bearing rather than decorative.

### The honest limit (must appear in the post)

The thermostat regulates **structural** entropy (does the registry match disk, do the artifacts exist). It cannot regulate **semantic** entropy (did the README get clearer or murkier). That floor is low. A green check is permission to proceed, not a certificate of meaning. The post must say this, or it overclaims.
