## The Red Sea Runtime Environment: A FAFO Case Study

Imagine the execution of this scenario in a contested bottleneck like the Bab-el-Mandeb or the Straits of Hormuz:

### 1. The Surface Profile (The Bait)

A lone Royal Navy Type 45 destroyer is stationed visible to all, actively escorting a convoy of commercial tankers or evacuating civilians from a coastal flashpoint. To shore-based insurgent batteries or hostile fast-attack craft, this ship looks isolated and target-rich. Its radar is lighting up the sky, making it an obvious target for a saturation strike.

### 2. The Asymmetric Trigger (The "Fuck Around" Phase)

A hostile shore-based actor decides to test the perimeter. They launch a coordinated, low-cost saturation attack: four truck-mounted anti-ship cruise missiles paired with a wave of low-altitude loitering drones. They expect to overwhelm the destroyer's limited, expensive air-defense silos (the Sea Viper system) through sheer volume.

### 3. The Subsea Relink (The "Find Out" Phase)

The destroyer’s radar tracks the inbound threat instantly, but instead of burning through its own defensive missiles and revealing its exact defensive limitations, it opens a secure, directional datalink. The telemetry is passed directly down to a converted Vanguard-class hull sitting completely undetected 40 miles away in deep water.

```
[Hostile Shore Battery] ──(Missile Strike)──> [Visible Type 45 Destroyer]
                                                    │
                                            (Target Telemetry)
                                                    │
                                                    ▼
[Target Liquidated] <──(Tomahawk/UAV Swarm)── [Hidden Subsea Mothership]

```

### 4. The Saturation Response

Before the adversary’s missiles have even cleared the coastal horizon, the submerged mothership responds. It doesn't fire at the inbound missiles; it fires at the **origin points**.

* Modular vertical tubes rapidly launch a devastating wave of conventional Tomahawk cruise missiles and smart loitering munitions.
* Because the sub is submerged and decoupled from the destroyer's location, the return fire catches the enemy completely by surprise from an entirely unexpected vector.

The mobile launch trucks, the command-and-control tents, and the drone assembly points are completely pulverized before their own initial salvo even reaches the destroyer’s electronic warfare jamming envelope.

### 5. The Mop-Up

The destroyer successfully swathes the remaining inbound threats out of the sky using its close-in weapon systems. Within minutes, the subsea mothership floods its converted missile bays, releasing a squadron of autonomous underwater drones and a Special Boat Service (SBS) team via its dry-deck shelters. They slip ashore under total radar darkness to assess the damage, disable leftover assets, and ensure the threat vector is permanently neutralized.
