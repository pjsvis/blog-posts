# *The Empty Quarter* by H. St. John Philby (1933)

## Full Title
*The Empty Quarter: Being a Description of the Great South Desert of Arabia Known as Rub' al Khali*

## Overview
This is the classic travel narrative and geographical account of **Harry St. John Bridger Philby** (often called "Abdullah Philby" after his conversion to Islam), the legendary British explorer, Arabist, and father of the infamous Cold War spy **Kim Philby**. Published in **1933** by Constable & Co. (London) and Henry Holt (New York), the book documents Philby's extraordinary **1932 crossing of the Rub' al Khali** — the "Empty Quarter" — the largest continuous sand desert in the world, covering approximately 250,000 square miles of southern Arabia.

## Key Content

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **The Crossing** | Philby led the first major European expedition across the Rub' al Khali, a journey of immense danger through shifting dunes, extreme heat (55°C+), and near-total water scarcity. |
| **Wabar Craters** | Philby was the first Westerner to describe the **Wabar meteorite craters** — the remnants of a dramatic iron meteorite impact that created fields of black glassy "pearls" (fulgurite) from melted sand. This discovery was later revisited by geologists E.M. Shoemaker and J.C. Wynn in 1995. |
| **The Legend of Ubar** | The expedition was partly motivated by the search for **Ubar** (Iram of the Pillars), the fabled lost city said to have been destroyed by divine punishment. Philby did not find the ancient city but encountered the meteorite strike site instead. |
| **Arabian Life & Politics** | As a former adviser to **King Ibn Saud** (the founder of Saudi Arabia), Philby wove rich ethnographic detail into the narrative — describing Bedouin tribes, desert survival techniques, and the turbulent politics of 1930s Arabia. |

## Significance

- **Pioneering Exploration**: It remains one of the foundational Western accounts of the Empty Quarter and a landmark in Arabian exploration literature.
- **Family Connection**: Philby's 21-year-old son **Kim Philby** (later one of the "Cambridge Five" spies) was employed by his father to do the **proofreading** of the manuscript — a task he performed "with care but not without error."
- **Cultural Legacy**: The book inspired the contemporary Kuwaiti artist **Monira Al Qadiri's** film and installation work *Holy Quarter* (2020), which reimagines Philby's expedition through a speculative, ecological lens, juxtaposing the meteorite "pearls" with warnings about environmental destruction.

## Style
The book blends **adventure narrative, scientific observation, and political memoir**. Philby writes with the authority of someone who lived among the Bedouin for years, spoke fluent Arabic, and had converted to Islam. It is both a gripping tale of survival and a serious geographical and ethnographic study.

> *"The Empty Quarter is a simply terrible place — a place where the sun is your enemy, the sand is your prison, and water is worth more than gold."* — paraphrasing the book's tone.

In short, it is a **seminal work of desert exploration literature** — part *Arabian Nights*, part scientific report, part personal odyssey — written by one of the 20th century's most fascinating (and controversial) figures in Middle Eastern affairs.
