The tragedy is that this institutional theater requires real-world funding, starving our domestic manufacturing sector of the resources needed to build actual conventional defenses. ### Tactical Notes on the Compilations

* **The Telegraph version** uses words like "hollowed out," "defensive weapons," and "adversary." It speaks the language of the defense realist who is worried about the actual state of the Navy and Army right now.
* **The Times version** uses words like "utilitarian value," "Whitehall paradox," and "institutional theater." It appeals directly to the policy wonks and civil servants who enjoy seeing a beautifully engineered bureaucratic contradiction laid bare.

Both versions firmly plant the "spite vs. character" flag right in the center of the page, forcing the reader to confront the actual human element behind the hardware.
